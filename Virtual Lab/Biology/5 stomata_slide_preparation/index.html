Stomata slide preparation

Objectives

To make a  temporary mounts of leaf peel to observe stomata in the leaf .

 

Theory

In all the autotrophic plants (green plants)gaseous exchange takes place through minute pores called stomata .These pores are present on the surface of the leaves. Stomata  are pores surrounded with two bean shaped gaurd cells on either sides

 The guard cells have thin outer and thick inner walls. When guard cells are filled with water they are called turgid, the stomata opens and it closes when the guard cells are water deficient (flaccid). The number, distribution and type of stomata varies in different plants.

Within a plant, the number and distribution may vary between the upper and lower surfaces of leaf. In this experiment we will observe a Rhoeo leaf .

 Dos & don’ts

Take a freshly plucked leaf.

Do not allow the leaf to dry .

Keep it wet in the watch glass.

Do not allow air bubbles in the slide.

Absorb the extra water and glycerine with blotting paper.
