Theory/Basic Principle involved

pH is the measure of hydrogen ion concentration.

Hydrogen ion concentration of a solution is equal to 10 to the power minus pH. i.e.

[H+] = 10-pH

pH can also be defined as:

“The negative logarithm(base 10) of the hydrogen ion concentration  (in moles per litre) is called pH”

pH = -log[H+]

Neutral solution has pH value 7. pH of acid solution is always less than 7,  whereas that of alkaline solution is always more than 7. 

pH of a sample can be measured by the use of pH paper or by the use of universal indicator.

solutions of different pH gives different colours on pH paper(or with universal indicator)
