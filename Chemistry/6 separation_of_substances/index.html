Separation of mixture of sand and ammonium chloride

OBJECTIVES:

1. To make pupil understand that mixtures can easily be separated by physical methods.

2. To acquaint the children about heterogeneous nature of mixtures.

3. To make the students understand about simple physical process sublimation to separate sublime from non sublime substance..

THEORY

Sand and ammonium chloride form a heterogeneous mixture and can be separated easily by physical methods of separation. By selecting the right order of methods of separation, the two can be easily separated:
(i) ammonium chloride sublimes on heating;
(ii) sand is left being non volatile.
