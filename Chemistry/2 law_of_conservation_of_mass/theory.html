Theory:

A chemical reaction is process by which one set of chemical substances is transformed to another.

Substances which chemically react are called reactants and the newly formed substances are called products.
A French chemist, Antoine Lavoisier, who is known as the father of modern chemistry, proved that the mass of the products in a chemical reaction is equal to the mass of the reactants. 

The Law of Conservation of Mass states that "matter can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction".

The following points can be keep into consideration:

1.    When reactants react, products are formed.

2.    As the reaction progresses, the mass of reactant decreases and the mass of the product increases.

3.    The sum of masses of the reactants consumed is equal to the sum of the masses of the products produced.

4.    The reaction between sodium sulphate and barium chloride is represented as

            Na2SO4(aq)+ BaCl2(aq) → BaSO4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)

5.     According to the law of conservation of mass

         Total mass of all the reactants consumed =  Total mass of all the products formed
